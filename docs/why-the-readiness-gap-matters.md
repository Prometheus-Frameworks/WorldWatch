# Why the Readiness Gap Matters

Many people see major events only after disruptions are already visible.

A readiness gap appears when:

- meaningful upstream signals exist,
- but they are too technical, fragmented, or late for non-specialists.

WorldWatch narrows that gap by translating live public-source signals into concise, plain-language summaries that support earlier awareness.

## Practical civilian value

- Better context for family, travel, and supply decisions.
- Faster recognition of uncertainty when data quality drops.
- More grounded conversations that separate observed changes from speculation.

## Principle

When evidence is incomplete, the right output is explicit uncertainty—not certainty theater.
